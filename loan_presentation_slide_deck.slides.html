WHY ARE LOANS NOT BEING COMPLETED?¶

by Amos Moses Omofaiye (UDACITY DAND SCHOLAR)¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this presentation I will show the factors that affect loan status. We all want loans to be paid on time. We don't like charging them off except when necessary. But at times we are just helpless. What can we do to forestall such occurrences? First we need to know WHY! I am here to show the WHY.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset is on Loan and their outcomes. The loan disbursing organization is Prosper Loan organizayion. This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan status, borrower APR (or interest rate), available bank card credit, employment status duration, listing category, borrower monthly income, and many others. The data cover loans from quarter 1 2007 to quarter 1 2014.

Borrower's Monthly Income Determines if they will complete their loans.¶

Borrowers that fall into the very_high income category have high bank card credit and higher rate of loan completion. Those with low income levels have less-favourable loan outcome.

Borrowers in Full time employment PAY BACK LOANS on TIME¶

Those borrowers who hold full-time employment fares better in paying back their loans. They have lower rates of defaults.

Short and Long Term loans are not easily recovered.¶

Favour medium term loans¶

Borrowers find it difficult to complete loans whose terms are above 36 months. Generally, loan terms of 36 months have better completion rate.

Insist on Recommendations¶

Borrowers who have at least one recommendation have a higher rate of completion than those who don't.

If such borrower has a high bank credit and a high monthly income, I can bet he/she will complete the loan even if the employment status duration is not so high

What should I do?¶

Now you know the why, then do these:

  • Insist that borrowers be employed
  • Check their monthly income and available bank card credit.
  • Let them have at least one recommendation.
  • Extend more short term loans of 36 months duration, and
  • Extend more loans to borrowers in the lower listing categories.

With these, you are on course to changing your story. I thank you.
